THE 10 PHASES

PHASE 1 — Foundation & Health Bars

Goal: Player and enemy entities live inside my existing loop with working
health bars.
Dependencies: Step 0 analysis.
Build:


Player and enemy entities with hp / max_hp, position, and base combat stats.
A reusable health bar that renders above any entity: a back bar, a fill bar
scaled to hp / max_hp, and color feedback as it drops. The player's bar also
renders in a fixed HUD position.
Pseudocode for taking damage, dying, and firing an on_death hook (so later
phases can attach XP, money, and loot to it).
Done when: player and enemies render, move, take damage, die, and show
correctly scaling health bars.


PHASE 2 — Combat Core & Resources

Goal: A real combat/resource model the rest of the game plugs into.
Dependencies: Phase 1.
Build:


A damage pipeline: base damage → modifiers → resistances → final damage.
Mana (mana / max_mana) and health/mana regeneration ticking per second.
Attack speed as a stat that gates how often the player can attack.
A money / gold currency tracked on the player.
A central Stats object on the player that everything else reads and writes,
so skill tree, gear, and ailments all funnel through one source of truth.
Done when: attacking consumes mana, regen ticks work, attack speed throttles
attacks, and gold can be gained/spent.


PHASE 3 — Leveling & Progression

Goal: Characters grow and earn skill points.
Dependencies: Phase 2.
Build:


XP gain from kills, an XP curve xp_to_next(level), and level-up handling.
On level-up: grant a passive skill point, raise core stats, raise/restore
health and mana, and surface a clear feedback cue.
Keep level / XP / skill-point state in one place the save system will read later.
Done when: kills grant XP, leveling raises stats and awards skill points.


PHASE 4 — PoE2-Style Passive Skill Tree

Goal: A meaningful, path-based passive tree that defines build identity and modifies build stat values.
Dependencies: Phase 3 (skill points), Phase 2 (Stats).
Build:


A graph of nodes (not a flat list): each node has an id, neighbor/edge list,
render position, a type (minor / notable / keystone), cost, and stat bonuses.
Path-based allocation (PoE2 rule): a node can only be allocated if it
connects to an already-allocated node. Spend skill points to allocate.
The tree must include nodes granting at minimum:

Move speed increases
Attack speed increases
Attack damage increases
Plus clustered/scaling versions (small nodes feeding a stronger notable).
Skill modifications and synergistic bonuses
Health regen increases
Mana regen increases




A StatAggregator that recomputes the player's effective stats by summing all
allocated node bonuses on top of base + (later) gear + (later) ailments.
Pseudocode for: building the tree from data, rendering it, hit-testing clicks,
allocating, and recomputing aggregated stats.
Done when: the player can spend points along connected paths and see move
speed / attack speed / attack damage change accordingly.


PHASE 5 — Rift & Greater Rift System  (the core endgame phase)

Goal: The full Diablo 3-style endless engine: normal rifts that earn
keystones, and player-chosen Greater Rifts that scale difficulty and reward.
Dependencies: Phases 1–4.
Build:


Boss pool of 10: define 10 distinct boss enemies (data-driven: hp, damage,
abilities, theme). At the end of every rift, one boss from the pool spawns
and, on death, rewards the player a large amount of XP and money.
Normal Rifts:

Procedurally assembled, endless-feeling encounter areas with spawning enemies.
A yellow progress bar that fills as enemies are killed — each enemy
killed contributes to rift progression. When the bar fills, the rift boss spawns.
Killing the normal rift boss rewards Rift Keystones (the gate to Greater Rifts).



Rift Keystones: consumable items held in inventory; required to open a Greater Rift.
Greater Rifts (GR):

Opened only by consuming a Rift Keystone.
The player chooses the GR level from 1 to 100 before opening, which
dictates both difficulty and reward magnitude (higher level = tougher
enemies + bigger XP/money/loot).
A purple progress bar (vs. the normal rift's yellow) that fills as enemies
are killed; filling it spawns the GR boss.
Define clearly tunable scaling formulas: enemy hp, damage, density, and
reward multipliers all as functions of GR level (1–100).



Pseudocode for: generating a rift, spawning waves, the kill→progress→boss
flow, the boss dropping keystones / XP / money, the inventory holding keystones,
and the "choose GR level → consume keystone → open scaled GR" flow.
Done when: the player clears a normal rift (yellow bar), kills its boss, gets
a keystone, picks a GR level 1–100, opens a GR (purple bar), and the GR boss
rewards XP/money scaled to the chosen level.

PHASE 7 — Vulnerable Status System

Goal: A debuff that rewards setup and feeds the loot loop.
Dependencies: Phases 5 & 6.
Build:


Enemies can be afflicted with Vulnerable. While Vulnerable:

They take an additional 15% damage (further increased by the player's
Increased Vulnerable damage stat from gear).
Their health bar turns purple to indicate the state.
They have a chance to drop rift progress orbs that correspond to the
current rift type (yellow orbs in normal rifts, purple orbs in greater rifts),
pushing the appropriate progress bar forward when collected.



Pseudocode for applying/refreshing/expiring Vulnerable, the damage hook that
adds the 15% (+gear) multiplier, the health-bar color swap, and the orb drop/pickup.
Done when: afflicted enemies show a purple bar, take extra damage, and can
drop type-matched progress orbs.


PHASE 8 — Elemental Skill Modifications I

Goal: Skills gain elemental behavior that creates ailments.
Dependencies: Phases 2, 4, 7.
Build:


A skill-modifier system so a skill can carry an element. Implement:

Fire → Burn: applies a damage-over-time burn stack to the target.
Ice → Slow & Freeze: slows movement/attack; enough buildup freezes
(briefly stuns) the enemy.
Shock → Static Chain: applies a static charge; lightning chains between
affected (shocked) enemies, hitting nearby charged targets.



Each ailment is data-driven (duration, magnitude, stack rules) and routes its
damage through the Phase 2 damage pipeline (so resistances and Vulnerable apply).
Done when: fire enemies burn over time, ice enemies slow/freeze, and shock
arcs between shocked enemies.


PHASE 9 — Elemental Skill Modifications II (Synergies & Depth)

Goal: Build on the ailments so they interact and scale, deepening buildcraft.
Dependencies: Phase 8 (and 4, 6, 7).
Build:


Tree + gear scaling of ailments: add passive nodes and affixes that boost
burn damage, freeze threshold/duration, and shock chain count/range.
Element interactions / combos, e.g.:

Frozen enemies take bonus hit damage (shatter-style payoff).
Burning + Vulnerable stacks multiply DoT.
Shock chains spread Vulnerable or refresh ailments across the chain.



Optional keystone-tier tree nodes that change how an element behaves (build-defining).
Define every interaction as tunable data so balance stays adjustable.
Done when: ailments scale off tree/gear, combo interactions fire, and distinct
elemental builds feel meaningfully different.


PHASE 10 — Save/Load, Endless Loop & Balance

Goal: Persist everything and lock in the endless, enjoyable loop.
Dependencies: All previous phases.
Build:


Save / Load to the project file. Serialize the full player state: level, XP,
current stats, allocated skill-tree nodes (by id), inventory (incl. keystones),
equipped gear, gold, and highest GR level cleared. Match my project's existing
format/location, or recommend one (e.g., a JSON save slot) and justify it.
Loading must rebuild the tree's allocated state and recompute aggregated stats
so a loaded character is identical to the saved one.
The endless loop, tied together: clear normal rift (yellow) → boss drops
keystone + XP/money → choose GR level 1–100 → open GR (purple) → clear → bigger
rewards + higher GR available → repeat, with leveling, gear, and ailment builds
growing the whole way.
Balance pass: centralize all tunable curves (XP, GR scaling, drop rates,
ailment numbers, Vulnerable %) and note pacing levers that keep it fun, not grindy.
Done when: a character can be saved and reloaded identically, and the full
rift → keystone → greater rift → reward → escalation loop runs endlessly.
